What are cancer Networks?

Collaborative systems involving Healthcare professionals, researchers, patients, policy makers, etc.

What do cancer Networks do?

These work together to address various aspects of cancer prevention, diagnosis, treatment and support.

Where are cancer Networks based?

These networks can take on various forms from local community based inititives to large international collaborations.

Cancer Networks

In the context of healthcare, cancer networks aim to improve the coordination and quality of cancer care by facilitating communication among different healthcare providers, ensuring that patients receive timely and comprehensive treatment. They also play a crucial role in disseminating knowledge about the latest advancements in cancer research and treatment modalities, helping to standardize best practices across institutions.
